1. How Facebook pays creators today

Previously, Meta had separate programs:

In-Stream Ads
Ads on Reels
Performance Bonus

These have largely been combined into Facebook Content Monetization.

This program pays for eligible:

Reels
Long videos
Photos
Text posts
Stories

The better your content performs, the more you earn.

2. Is it available to everyone?

No.

Currently Facebook Content Monetization is still invite-only in many regions.

You cannot simply click a button and join.

Instead:

Create a Facebook Page or Professional Profile.
Grow your audience.
Meet Meta's requirements.
Receive an invitation or express interest through the Professional Dashboard.
3. Basic eligibility

Generally you must:

Be 18+
Live in an eligible country
Follow Community Standards
Follow Partner Monetization Policies
Follow Content Monetization Policies
Use original content
Complete tax and payment setup once invited.
4. The most important rule: Original content

Meta rewards original creators, not people who copy videos.

Good examples:

You film your own videos.
You record your own voice.
You create tutorials.
You review products.
You make travel videos.
You edit your own footage.

Bad examples:

Downloading TikTok videos.
Re-uploading YouTube videos.
Movie clips.
Cricket highlights.
TV scenes.
Anime clips.
Viral videos without permission.
AI-generated spam with little original value.

Repeated reposting can remove monetization and reduce distribution.

Meta checks:

Authentic followers
No fake engagement
No bought followers
No engagement groups
No view bots
Consistent original posting
Real audience growth

Buying followers or views is one of the fastest ways to lose eligibility.

7. How Facebook decides earnings

Facebook doesn't pay per follower.

It looks at:

Qualified views
Watch time
Audience engagement
Shares
Comments
Retention
Originality
Advertiser suitability

Recent updates also show creators metrics such as Qualified Views, Earnings Rate, and Non-Qualified Views.

8. Does follower count matter?

Followers help, but they're not everything.

A page with:

10,000 followers
Millions of monthly views

can often earn more than a page with:

500,000 inactive followers.

Views and engagement are more important than raw follower count.

Yes, if it is genuinely original and valuable.

Examples:

AI-assisted editing
AI voice with your own script
AI-generated graphics you meaningfully use

Avoid mass-produced or repetitive AI videos that add little value.
